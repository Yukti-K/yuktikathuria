I am currently a first year PhD student in Industrial Engineering at University of Massachusetts Amherst, and I am fortunate to be advised by Muge Capan. My research is focused on understanding health inequities that prevent vulnerable populations from timely access to healthcare and lead to certain communities experiencing a disproportionate burden of sickness/illness/death. My work involves developing analytical and computational models to risk stratify disadvantaged populations for resource allocation/targeted interventions.

Prior to joining University of Massachusetts Amherst, I graduated from University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign with a BS and MS in Aerospace Engineering.
