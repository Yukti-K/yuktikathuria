I am currently a second year Ph.D. student in Industrial Engineering at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, and I am advised by Muge Capan. My research is focused on understanding health inequities that prevent vulnerable populations from timely access to healthcare and lead to certain communities experiencing a disproportionate burden of sickness or death. My work involves developing analytical and computational models to risk stratify disadvantaged populations for decisions about resource allocation and targeted interventions.

Prior to joining the University of Massachusetts Amherst, I graduated from the University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign with a BS and MS in Aerospace Engineering.
